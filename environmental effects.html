Ethical Issues

As technological devices are being produced much more cheaply and widely,
they have become affordable for a large proportion of the world population.
The downside to this is the effect on the environment. The pressure of
keeping up with trends, alongside the affordability, means that people now
throw away more devices than before. Some computer components are built
out of mercury and radioactive isotopes which are toxic and can contaminate
water supplies. Often, this waste is shipped and disposed of to third world
countries with lower environmental standards, which is considered to be
immoral and unacceptable.

That being said, the manufacturing process is one of the main ways that
technology can have a negative effect on the environment. There can be a
lot of wastage created during the construction of components, as well as the
emission from all transportation of each component from different factories.
he other way technology can have a negative impact on the environment is
that some systems, like web servers or domain name servers, require to be
left running constantly which can be quite energy demanding. Overall,
technology can add to the effects of global warming through the wastage
during manufacturing and its energy consumption, especially for larger
companies.

As more devices become readily available, and processes are becoming
either partially computerised or entirely computerised, more electricity is
required to power these devices. This requires using up our stores of non-
renewable fossil fuels which emit greenhouse gases into the atmosphere,
contributing to global warming. The rate of climate change has accelerated
over the past year, indicating that we need to be more careful about our
consumption when it comes to technological devices. As a result of our
consumption, the effects on the planet will go on to impact future
generations, as well as biodiversity.

environment:

  1. Avoid wasting electricity:
    • Turn off your computer and monitor when not in use
    • Use power management settings which are available on most newer
      computers. Power management settings determine when the
      computer and monitor shut off, or go into sleep or hibernate modes,
      which use less power than regular operating mode
    • Turn off peripherals, such as printers, scanners, speakers, external
      drives, and gaming systems, when not in use
    • Use a laptop or tablet instead of a desktop. Laptops typically
      consume less power than desktops, and tablets even less
  2. Avoid wasting paper:
    • Print only what is necessary
    • Share information electronically, and read on the screen when you
      can
    • Finish editing first rather than printing early drafts if you need a
      hard copy, and print on the fewest number of pages possible
  3. Dispose of old equipment responsibly:
    • Computers, monitors, and other peripheral devices frequently
      contain toxic materials, such as lead and mercury, so you should
      never throw old equipment in a landfill site
