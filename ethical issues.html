Censorship and the Internet
Censorship is the act of suppressing the communication and sharing of
knowledge, specifically by a government or private institution. The main
debate over censorship is the balance between public interest and protecting
the privacy of individuals and national security.

Political opinions have become an area of contention within censorship on
the internet as some argue you should be free to say whatever you want,
whilst others say that it is unethical and dangerous to allow extremist views
and ideologies to be published online, especially when targeted at younger
audience. ISP (Internet Service Providers) can often block such beliefs.

The idea of a ‘Free Internet’, where nothing is censored at all, in line with
the principle of freedom of speech. Tim Berners-Lee created the internet to
be a space where people could share and mutually benefit from resources.
However most people agree that some extent of censorship has become
necessary for national security purposes and to filter offensive comments and
extremist propaganda.

Monitoring Behaviour
Computers are used to monitor people’s behaviour in various environments.
In workplaces, employers may monitor productivity by tracking the websites
and applications workers are accessing. Surveillance systems and in
particular, CCTV cameras are widely used for security purposes and to detect
and deter crime. This is useful for tracing and punishing criminal activity.
Ankle monitors are another kind of surveillance device used on people under
house arrest to track their location. While some people argue that this is
unethical and contravenes basic human rights, others argue that this is a
necessary measure that must be taken to put people off committing crime.
This is another example of a confliction between human rights caused by the
growing dependence on technology.

Analyse Personal Information
Data mining is when large sums of data, or ‘big data’ is collected from
individuals and is analysed to make inferences about people’s behaviour, for
example likes and dislikes. Data mining is often used to inform personalised
political propaganda, targeted advertising or alternatively, to identify
previously unknown connections between two variables.

The main ethical issue raised by this is the exploitation of the data collected
and analysed by companies in a malicious way. For example, data mining
uncovering that a certain individual is at high risk of developing a particular
disease. An insurance company could then deny them insurance or charge a
higher premium. This raises ethical questions about whether companies
uncovering these trends have a responsibility to feed this information back to
users. Most companies must state what kind of data they are collecting from
users, but this may not necessarily be made clear. The Data Protection Act of
1989 no longer effective protecting people from large companies exploiting
their data as it predates many of the large companies accused of misusing
data mining, so the GDPR act came into force in 2018.

Piracy and Offensive Communications
Piracy is the unauthorised copying of content, such as software or media
(including music and films). This is a form of theft and is illegal, although the
internet has made it easier to source these types of content online. It is up
to the individual to make the decision as to whether or not this is ethically
correct.

While the internet has opened up countless positive pathways for the
betterment of society, it also provides an anonymous front for people.
Offensive communication refers to any sort of online harassment, including
cyber-bullying or stalking. The 1998 Malicious Communications Act makes it a
criminal offence to send indecent or offensive messages to anyone online,
and this sort of behaviour can be traced by law enforcement organisations
and result in a criminal record.

Layout, Colour Paradigms and Character Sets
When web developers design websites, it is important that they consider who
will be viewing the website to ensure the website is designed to
accommodate for all of these people. The 2010 Equality Act makes it illegal
to discriminate against providing a service to a certain group of people.

Websites must be laid out in a way that makes it easy for users to navigate
between pages. Menus are a common tool used to provide function and in
English-speaking countries, menus are displayed on the left-hand side of the
page. On the other hand, in countries such as Egypt or the United Arab
Emirates (where Arabic is the primary language), these menus may instead
be displayed on the right-hand side of the page as Arabic is read from right to
left. These design considerations must be made by developers in order to
make websites accessible to all people.

Websites must also be designed in a way that makes them accessible to
people with visual impairments or other disabilities. Some people may need
to enlarge text or alter the contrast in order to view the contents of the
website comfortably. Websites should provide alternative text (alt text) for
images and provide a screen magnifier option so that people with visual
impairments are not disadvantaged. Transcripts of audio files should be
provided for those with hearing impairments.

When choosing a colour scheme for a website, web developers must take into
account how different colours are interpreted around the world. Some
colours are regarded as unlucky in certain cultures and have negative
connotations. For example, white is associated with mourning in the Middle
East, but it is associated with purity in Western cultures. Typically, a neutral
colour scheme with widely positive connotations will be chosen, such as
green to represent luck and nature.

In order to make websites accessible to as wide an audience as possible, the
contents must be translated into multiple languages. Some character sets are
too small to accommodate all of the characters of a language. ASCII only uses
seven bits and so is unable to represent all of the characters in the Chinese
language. Therefore, Unicode is the preferred character set as it is able to
represent over a million characters.
