In recent years there has been a growing dependency on technology to
function in society. The workplace is no exception, with many companies
with many companiesâ€™ success being dependant on their prevalent access to
technology and computers. As a result, computers have become an important
part of how companies conduct business as well as how workers perform
their job tasks, as well as impact where employees do their jobs.

There are many reasons as to why the usage of computers in the workforce
are deemed as advantageous:

  1. Improving communication:
    When within the workplace and dealing with customers, computers can
    be used to improve communication in both cases. For example, an email
    can convey a large amount of information at one time whilst being
    viewed at the convenience of the recipient. This can eliminate the
    slowness of sending written information via postal mail.
  2. Improving efficiency:
    Efficiency is improved in the workforce due to computers being able to
    increase the speed and accuracy of many work processes. For example,
    documents can be written and edited much more quickly with a word
    processing program, and procedures, such as billing and accounting, can
    also occur more rapidly and with fewer errors. Computers also help
    companies in administrative tasks, such as keeping up-to-date and accurate records.
  3. Less paper:
    As workplaces grow more dependent on computers, their need for paper
    may decrease. Some companies may even introduce a policy of
    eliminating the use of paper wherever possible. Reducing paper may
    lead to a reduction in office supply costs, or even open more work space
    that was previously used to store paper documents in filing cabinets.

Consequently, there are equally as many reasons as to why the usage of
computers in the workforce are viewed as disadvantageous:

  1. Hardware and software concerns:
    Reducing the use of paper in the workforce is only as effective as the
    hardware and software used to store the records allow the workers to
    be. The maintenance of the hardware and software of a paper-reducing
    office is also a concern as information technology (IT) staff or
    consultants are often a necessity, as well as technicians to service the
    hardware on a regular basis.
  2. Data entry errors and learning curves:
    Incorporating technology into a workforce often involves a learning
    curve, especially in an environment where employees are not already
    tech savvy. Data entry errors can be costly and can result in major
    problems, and data entry and filing errors can result in miscategorising
    records, making it difficult or impossible to find them at a later date.
  3. Security concerns:
    Digital records can be vulnerable to unauthorised access. Especially with
    sensitive legal and medical records, data loss can result in significant
    liability for the company involved. The disposing of digital records
    presents a special difficulty, especially in a shared network environment.
    Simply erasing a file does not completely delete a digital record as it
    does not prevent someone else from retrieving the file later.
