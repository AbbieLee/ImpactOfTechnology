Artificial Intelligence (commonly abbreviated to ‘AI’) is the perceiving,
synthesizing, and inferring of information by machines, as opposed to
intelligence displayed by humans or animals. It displays the human-like
capabilities such as reasoning, learning, and planning.

The applications for artificial intelligence are endless, as the technology can
be applied to many different sectors and industries. They can be found
within:

  • Web search engines (Google)
  • Recommendation systems (YouTube, Amazon, Netflix)
  • Understanding human speech (Siri, Alexa)
  • Generative or creative tools (ChatGPT, AI art)
  • Healthcare industry (suggesting drug dosages, identifying treatments,
    aiding in surgical procedures in the operating room)
  • Computers that weigh the consequences of any action taken (chess, self-driving cars)

The advantages of artificial intelligence are significant and can revolutionise
any professional sector. These include:

  1. Reduction in human error:
    Computers do not make the mistakes humans make if they are
    programmed properly. With artificial intelligence, the decisions are
    taken from the previously gathered information, which is then applied
    by a certain set of algorithms. Errors are reduced and the chance of
    reaching accuracy with a greater degree of precision is a possibility.
  2. Availability:
    The average person will work for four-to-six hours a day, excluding the
    breaks. Humans are built in such a way to get some time off for
    refreshing themselves and get ready for a new day of work, as well as
    taking longer breaks to stay intact with both their work-life and their
    personal life. But on the other hand, artificial intelligence can work
    twenty-four-seven without any breaks or getting bored, unlike humans.
  3. Digital assistance:
    Some of the most highly advanced organisations use digital assistants to
    interact with users, saving the need for human resources. The digital
    assistants used in many websites can provide the things that users want.
    We can chat with them about what we are looking for. Some chatbots
    are designed in such a way that it become hard to determine whether or
    not we’re chatting with a chatbot or a human being.
  4. Daily applications:
    Applications such as Apple’s Siri, Window’s Cortana, and Google’s "OK
    Google" are frequently used in our daily lives whether it is for searching
    a location, taking a selfie, making a phone call, replying to an email,
    and much more.

Alongside its many controversial and topical discussions over artificial
intelligence, there are as many disadvantages as there are advantages. Some
of them are as follows:

  1. High costs of creation:
    As artificial intelligence is updating every day, the hardware and
    software need to be updated with time in order to meet the latest
    requirements. Machines need repairing and maintenance, which require
    plentiful in costs. Its creation also requires huge costs as they are very
    complex machines.
  2. Laziness of human beings:
    Artificial intelligence is making humans lazy with its applications
    automating the majority of the work. Humans may also tend to get
    addicted to these inventions, which can cause a problem to future
    generations. For example, students of current and upcoming generations
    may become more dependent on chatbots for their educational studies.
  3. Unemployment:
    As artificial intelligence is replacing the majority of the repetitive tasks
    and other works involving robots, human interference is becoming less
    which will cause a major problem in the employment standards.
    Nowadays, organisations look to replace the minimally qualified
    individuals with artificial intelligence robots, which can do similar work
    with more efficiency.
